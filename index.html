CONCLAVE

The gathering of the intellectual cream of the nation for the yearly sessions of unparalleled brain-storming and exchange of ideas, is back again this year. With the theme being BRIDGING THE GAP, it proposes great prospects of discussion on technology and entrepreneurship

MOTIVE

♦ An aspect of this event is the effective sharing of knowledge, ideas and resource base to bring all the NITs at a uniform level of development, upholding the very concept of inclusive growth -the primary strategy for genuine progress of a nation.


♦ Innovation, creativity and cumulative strength are necessary to build brand NIT and NIT Conclave targets the participation and contribution of the new species of optimistic &ambitious young engineers, the future harbingers of progress for our country to plant a seedling for such a symbiotic relationship amongst NITs that can sustain itself for the years to come and contribute to the greater cause of nation building.

SPEAKERS

Dr. Anil Kumar Gupta

Dr. Anil Kumar Gupta:

Dr. Anil Kumar Gupta is a professor in the Centre for Management in Agriculture at Indian Institute of Management, Ahmedabad. He is also the founder of the Honey Bee Network and a fellow of the World Academy of Art and Science. Besides, he holds the Executive vice Chair of the National Innovation Foundation. He is also the Coordinator of SRISTI (Society for Research and Initiative for Sustainable Technologies and Institutions). He was a speaker at TED India in November, 2009. Since 2011, he is an advisor on issues pertaining to innovation, environment, and sustainability to FAIR OBSERVER, an online magazine covering global issues.

After finishing his BSc (Hons) in Agriculture, he went on to complete his MSc (Biochemical Genetics)from Haryana Agricultural University in 1974. In 1986, he earned a PhD in Management from Kurukshetra University.

His interests include ensuring recognition, respect and reward for grassroots inventors and innovators at a local, national and global level, and protection of their Intellectual Property Rights. Amalgamation of formal and informal science, ethical issues in conservation and prosperity of biodiversity are some of his other research interests. Every summer and winter, in order to learn from grassroots teachers, he has been walking around 6000 km across India for over a week as part of Shodh Yatra for more than twelve years. In fact, Shodh Yatra, which is a second year course, is one of the most popular courses taught in IIM Ahmedabad. Through the Honey Bee Network, he has demonstrated the pivotal role of innovative ideas and knowledge, possessed by economically poor people, in the sustainable progress of developing countries.

Mr. Hiranmay Mahanta

Mr. Hiranmay Mahanta:

Mr. Hiranmay Mahanta, a 2010 batch SVNIT pass-out, is currently leading national knowledge portal techpedia.in at SRISTI based in Ahmedabad. As a strong believer of individual institution making mission for vision 2020, he has played a critical role in  the development of GTU Innovation Council which involves more than 3 lakh youths in  various stream of professional education. He has led the efforts of forging linkage between industries-academia and innovations and set up a model in Gujarat which is now being tried to be adopted in Karnataka and Punjab.

Mr. Rajiv Vaishnav

Mr. Rajiv Vaishnav:

Mr. Rajiv Vaishnav is the Vice President – Member Outreach at NASSCOM, the premier trade body and the ‘Chamber of Commerce’ for the IT-BPO industry in India. He is responsible for member outreach and is simultaneously leading several forums at NASSCOM. As part of the member outreach, he conceptualizes and implements the development of multiple touch points for members by synergizing NASSCOM and its member companies’ interests and objectives, at both the regional and national levels.Additionally, he also leads the Remote Infrastructure Management (RIM) Forum, Emerging Companies Forum, Product Forum, Engineering Services Forum, Animation Forum, Quality Forum, Captive and the Women in Leadership initiatives.

Rajiv has been with NASSCOM for six years, and has rolled out NASSCOM’s Western Region operations with setting up of its first regional office in Mumbai, and later the Pune office. As the Regional Director for Western Region, he has initiated several state level activities and events in Maharashtra, Gujarat, Madhya Pradesh, Chhatisgarh and Goa. He has been an instrumental part of NASSCOM’s Trusted Sourcing Initiative and has led the Cyber Labs initiative in Mumbai, Thane and Pune in partnership with the enforcement agencies.

His previous work experience of nearly 25 years spans across the corporate and not for profit sector, he was the Director, TiE Mumbai before joining NASSCOM and played a key role in establishing the organisation grass-root upwards in India. He has worked with Indo-American Chamber of Commerce (IACC), Franchising Association of India (FAI) and US- Environment Resource Center (US-ERC). In the corporate sector, his experience includes stints with Emery Worldwide and Air Express International.Rajiv is a Science graduate from Mumbai University, and is very passionate about realising the potential of the Indian IT industry.

Prof. U T RAO

Prof. U T Rao:

Mr. U T Rao is an assistant professor of Finance and General Management at MICA, Ahmedabad. He has an academic experience of 10 years and industrial experience of 5 years. He is the head of the COMCUBATOR (Communication Technology Based Incubator) and is the adviser to the Director on Finance and Human Resources. His teaching and research interests include Financial Systems, MIS and Business Strategy. He is currently serving on the board of Shrey Multifacility, Solitaire Remedies and MTR Technologies.

Academics, Research and T&P

These 3 seemingly simple words are the gospel for any engineering college and for us NITans, it is no different. Being premier engineering colleges and Institutes of National Importance, all NITs are supposed to set a benchmark for others in the said fields. Producing top- class academicians and scholars, providing state-of-the-art technology and equipment for groundbreaking research and providing a platform for their students to get the best training and invaluable placements, are some of the aims of an NIT and they strive hard to achieve them. This session will provide an insight, for everyone, into the workings of the NITs in these fields. Students will present a scenario of their college in these areas; their pros, cons and the scope for improvement. Rest assured! This session will provide an amazing level of understanding that students should have of the intricate functioning of their college!

Learn how LinkedIn's mobile web app manages control flow and application structure with promises and page controller components. As is the case with many original applications, the code-base of LinkedIn's first mobile web app was a bit like the "wild west." It was, in some areas, ill-suited to support the requirements of a large mobile web application that was constantly evolving at the hands of several teams. This session will share the problems faced, lessons learned, and solutions found while developing and scaling the LinkedIn mobile web app. After giving a quick primer on promises and explaining what the heck controllers are for, the session will dive into the use of promises to manage control flow and page controllers to manage page cycle, data, and memory.
